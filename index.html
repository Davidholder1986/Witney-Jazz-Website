What’s On

Rob Terry Trio with ‘The Greatness of Gershwin’

September 8th 2016

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Sublime piano led trio creates a unique performance of Gershwin’s classical & jazz music - “Who needs an orchestra when these three are playing?” Annie’s Jazz

Read MORE Book

Rob and his trio have taken Mr Gershwin's jazz AND classical compositions and re-arranged them for piano, double bass and drums - the result is extraordinary! Combining a familiar jazz standards repertoire with western classical music in a way that truly works - Rob Terry's music features melody and gripping dynamics, beautifully played. Their performances simply 'drip' with virtuosity, yet this is no pompous 'conceptual' band playing self indulgent originals. Rob, Paul and Charlie are definitely out to entertain. There are self penned pieces, but these are imbued with a sense of familiarity that renders them truly engaging. A delight........

Roger Beaujolais Band

October 7th 2016

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Roger’s work as possibly the UK’s finest (certainly busiest) vibes player is legendary - "There’s enormous craft (and soul) in deceptively simple music like this” - Jazz UK

Read MORE Book

Composer, educator and revered musician, Roger is one of the UK’s most respected vibes players and a champion for UK Jazz. Now you may have hear Mr B perform before (he’s a regular at most of the Jazz club’s up and down the land) but this time Roger is assembling a new band to perform a concert that focuses on his original music. fear not standards lovers as he’ll be peppering the show with well known pieces - there’ll be a full ‘spread’ of styles from Latin, through blues to straight ahead swinging jazz - all of it foot-tapping and brimming with entertaining fizz!

Stuart Henderson Quartet

November 11th 2016

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Here’s a man who can blow his own horn! Mr Henderson’s music brims with excitement and passion - “A player of outstanding quality” - Jazz in reading

Read MORE Book

This man Henderson is truly a remarkable musician. One time ‘Royal’ trumpeter, he's created an enviable reputation for himself in the UK as a performer of the highest calibre, jazz educator and composer. It's easy to draw comparisons with the greats, but Stuart's can be pitched somewhere between the warm joyous tones of Chet Baker and the hard Bop of Lee Morgan. Stylistically you cannot pigeon hole Mr Henderson, for Witney Jazz he'll be playing a selection of delicious, well known standards with a distinctly funky edge. On hand to assist with creating that all important rhythm will be hard hitting guitarist Hugh Turner, powerhouse bassist Paul Jefferies and a drummer of distinction - Simon Price.

Ben Holder’s Christmas Special

December 8th 2016

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Ben brings a festive extravaganza to Witney Jazz - if you go to one Christmas concert, try to make it this one! - “Possibly the most exciting jazz violinist in Europe today” - Pershore Jazz Festival

Read MORE Book

Ben kicked off our very first concert at Witney jazz and created a real stir! His energy is phenomenal, his playing at speed or in a gentle ballad is simply mind boggling and he just keeps pressing the entertainment buttons throughout any concert that he stars in. SO, we’re delighted to bring him back but this time with pianist Rob Terry and guitarist Jez Cook and bassist Paul Jefferies to create some really new sounding jazz which celebrates this festive time of year. There’ll be nods to the classical world, beautiful arrangements of well known carols, christmassy jazz standards and a smattering of originals from Ben and Rob for you to get your teeth into.

Tom Pilling Trio
'Oscar’s Night'

6th January 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Oxford boy made good in the ‘smoke’ Tom Pilling nods with much respect to the music of Oscar Peterson - “Terrific pianist with a heartfelt voice - and the other two aren’t bad either” - Jazz-on-Tap, Burton

Read MORE Book

Grab a glass, pull up a chair and strap yourself in for an evening of toe-tappingly fine jazz, celebrating the music of the 1960s Oscar Peterson Trio - it's Oscar's night at Witney Jazz. Welcome - sadly not - the iconic figure (Oscar) himself, but a very worthy emulator in the more svelte shape of celebrated London based pianist Tom Pilling with Paul Jefferies sitting in for the great Ray Brown and ace midlands based drummer Nick Millward working the Ed Thigpen kit. There will be shed loads of swing; there will be shockingly good ballads and these three maestros will play through two sets of truly inspiring tunes - pieces that Oscar's Trio became famous for.

Tina May Quartet

February 3rd 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

The delightful doyen of British Jazz Tina May, has mesmerised jazz fans, theatres and francophiles throughout the world - come catch some mesmerisation!

Read MORE Book

Trying to squeeze in here even a snapshot of Tina’s career and the accolades that follow her performances is tough! She’s performed and often recorded with just about everyone on the British, US and Euro scenes who is anyone, but we can tell you that her voice is utterly captivating - as is her presence and charisma on stage. Pin-point accurate pitch, immaculate phrasing and a vocal sound that’s both rich and smooth but with a hint of huskiness. So expect some scintillating jazz from Tina and a rather tight rhythm section of Rob Terry (piano), Paul jefferies (bass) and Charlie Stratford (drums).
